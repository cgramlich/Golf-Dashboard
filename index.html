📍 Ball Position by Club
Discipline: ball position is a fixed fundamental — measured and identical every time, not a feeling. It does not drift back on its own. If it moved, you moved it
Driver: Off left heel — ball just forward of the tee peg (hitting on the upswing). Tee at 1.5" (equator of ball at top of clubface)
3-wood (fairway): Just inside left heel — 1–2" right of driver position
5-wood (fairway): Between left heel and center — slightly more back than 3-wood
3-wood off tee: Just inside left heel — ball directly over the tee peg (level sweep). Tee ½" high, bottom of ball even with top of clubface
5-wood off tee: Between left heel and center — ball just behind the tee peg (slight downward strike). Tee ¼" high, bottom of ball at top of tee (bottom groove of clubface contacts equator of ball)
Mid irons (5–7): Just left of center
Short irons (8–PW): Center or just right of center
52° (gap wedge): Center or just right of center — similar to PW
56° (sand wedge): Just right of center — encourages a slightly descending strike
60° (lob wedge): Just right of center — ball back promotes clean contact and prevents fat shots
🧍 Address Cues (Brian Norman)
Weight slightly forward — favor the lead side at address. This is the weight only — not the head
Head behind the ball — "nose over" is an anti-sway cue only: keep the head still and behind the ball, never let it drift in front of it
Spine square — not tilting sideways
Right shoulder — aim it at the target as your directional guide
Don't sway right — stay centered, left leg ready to post

🤝 Grip
Left hand — in the fingers: the club runs across the base of the fingers and under the heel pad — never buried in the palm
Left-hand V: the V formed by thumb and forefinger points to the right side of your chin
Two knuckles: looking down at address you see two knuckles of the left hand — no more, no less
Set it once: the grip is a fixed position, not a feeling. Set it before the session, check it, and never re-grip or re-work it between shots
Right thumb: more along the shaft than across it — reduces resistance and hand strain

💭 My Current Swing Thoughts
1. Feel weight of right foot pushing into the ground — prevents swaying off the ball
2. Head stays behind the ball — "nose over" only means do not sway off it. Keep the head still and behind the ball, never in front
3. Start the downswing before reaching the top — initiate body rotation while backswing is still completing
4. Right shoulder comes around on plane to target — drives through the ball, not past it
5. Keep clubface square and in line with spine — maintain face angle through impact
6. Rotate, don't swing the backswing — feel the torso turning, right shoulder stretching toward the target at full turn
7. Get arms up higher earlier — feel like lifting a waiter's tray, not dragging the club slowly away from the ball
8. Visualize the divot in front of the ball — from setup and takeaway, see the low point ahead of the ball. Let a body-generated swing drive through to create it, not hands
9. Left leg anchors the turn — coil into the stretch, let it unwind itself — left leg is the fixed post. Rotate against it on the backswing, then inertia does the work

⚡ Brian Norman Keys
Rotate body, not hands — let body drive the club, not hand manipulation
Left leg posts up — firm up the left side through impact
Thumbs to target before release — left arm straight, rotates through shoulder. Delay the hands
Sternum stays upright through downswing — if you dip, hands take over to compensate and you lose rotation
Right hand gets vertical on descent — club getting behind and too low causes a sweeping around motion. Feel the right hand drive vertically down
Shoulder rotates down and left — not just around. Down and left pulls the club through on plane
Hammer to nail: Handle leads through impact — if it was a hammer hitting a nail, it has to stay on path. Let it unwind and lead through the follow through
Fairway woods low point: Ball just inside left lead shoulder — good indicator of swing low point
🏋️ Drills
Split grip drill — feel rotation, minimize hand flip, sense of pulling club down
One-arm drill: Right hand only with left arm against chest or behind back. Use body to follow through. 10 reps, gradually add balls
Rotation drill: Trail arm connected to body, preset club shallow — stops hand flip at impact
Stop flip with broom: Hold broom through impact to eliminate flip
Half backswing drill: Short backswing, focus purely on shoulder rotation — feel like hitting outside of ball as shoulders turn through. Grooves rotation without arms taking over
Two-ball drill: Place a second ball just in front of and slightly inside your target ball — forces a more vertical downswing and stops club getting behind you. Hit the target ball without touching the front ball
Weighted ball toss feel: Imagine tossing a weighted ball with both hands — but use hips to rotate and power it, not arms. That's the sequencing feel

Don't stop quick after impact — keep rotating to a full finish

🦶 Toe-Down Chip — Mickelson (Bad Lies)
When to use: Tight, thin, or buried lies where getting the full leading edge under the ball is risky
The idea: Tip the club so only the toe contacts the ground — that smaller surface slides under the ball far more easily than the full leading edge
Setup: Shaft as vertical as possible — stand close, ball near your toe so only the toe points down
Swing: Let the toe fall straight underneath the ball — soft, simple motion, no manipulation
Key adjustment: Not hitting the sweet spot — ball comes out slower, so use a little more pace than normal
Toe-Down Chip ▶ — Phil Mickelson via NuclrGolf
🟡 Bunker Play
Ball position: Off left heel
Setup: Open stance and open face before gripping — grip down slightly
The swing thought: Sweep the sand out from under the ball — not digging in. Use the bounce, not the leading edge
Entry point: Just behind the ball — let the bounce slide through the sand
Accelerate through — never decelerate in the sand. Maintain speed all the way through
Practice drill: Take practice swings hitting sand out of bunker without a ball — build the feel of sweeping, not digging
